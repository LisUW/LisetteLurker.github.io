Research Interests

My research interests focus on human-centered technology, particularly in human-computer interaction, accessibility, robotics, educational technology, and human-AI interaction. I am interested in how interactive systems can support learning, inclusion, trust, and meaningful human experiences.

I am especially interested in how people engage with intelligent systems and how technology can be designed to be both technically effective and understandable to diverse users and communities.

Experience and Evidence

BioRobotics Lab Experience (UNAM)

I gained early research experience at the BioRobotics Laboratory at the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM), where I contributed to robotics education and team development within a research-oriented environment.

In this role, I was responsible for training and guiding junior robotics teams, focusing on foundational concepts in programming, problem-solving, and competition preparation. While I did not directly work on the primary research robot Justina, my work supported the development of students who could later approach more advanced robotics projects within the lab.

The lab’s work, including the Justina robot, appeared in public media coverage: View article

This experience introduced me to research environments at an early stage, where I learned to work in technically demanding contexts and developed resilience, adaptability, and collaborative skills.

Robotics Training, Teaching, and Competition

I have participated in robotics training and teaching environments focused on rapid learning, problem-solving, and competitive performance. These experiences included collaboration with robotics educator Emmanuel Merchán and work with students across different levels.

I also contributed to junior robotics training and mentorship connected to RoboCup Junior. In this work, I helped students build strong foundations that supported high-level competition performance.

Human-Centered Technology and Communication

In addition to technical work, I explore how complex technological ideas can be communicated through media and storytelling. This interest supports my broader research goal of making technology more accessible and meaningful to different audiences.

My approach to research combines technical understanding with communication, inclusion, and user-centered design.

Research Areas

  • Human-Computer Interaction (HCI): Study of how people interact with technology and how to design intuitive and meaningful systems.
  • Accessibility: Designing inclusive technologies that support diverse users and abilities.
  • Educational Robotics: Using robotics to support learning, creativity, and problem-solving.
  • Human-AI Interaction: Understanding how people engage with intelligent systems and how trust is shaped through design.

Future Goals

I plan to continue developing these interests through graduate study and future research in human-centered technology and interactive systems. My long-term goal is to contribute to research that improves access, learning, and inclusion through thoughtful technological design.

I am particularly interested in building a research path that connects technical systems with education, accessibility, and real human needs.
