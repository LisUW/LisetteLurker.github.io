AI and Cybersecurity: Expert Perspectives

Project type: Interview video

AI and Cybersecurity: Expert Perspectives is an interview-based video project built around a conversation with Dr. Rahul Chatterjee, a UW–Madison computer science faculty member specializing in security and privacy.

The project examines how everyday use of generative AI tools can unintentionally compromise cybersecurity, especially when users are unaware of data storage practices, network risks, and platform behavior. One of the goals of the interview was to make these risks feel concrete and understandable for a general audience rather than abstract or overly technical.

I developed the interview questions, structured the conversation around real-world user concerns, and edited the final video to highlight the most accessible and relevant expert insights. By translating technical research into a more human-centered format, the project connects expert knowledge with public understanding.

This project reflects my interest in critical digital studies, ethical technology use, and making complex technical issues understandable through media.

Equalizing Citizenship: Breaking Stigmas

Project type: Documentary-style video interview

Equalizing Citizenship: Breaking Stigmas is a documentary-style interview project that challenges common misconceptions about U.S. naturalization by centering lived experience and intergenerational memory.

Through an interview with Araceli Esparza, the project traces her family’s migration history, including her grandfather’s participation in the Bracero Program and her aunt’s path to U.S. citizenship. Rather than presenting citizenship as a simple legal outcome, the video highlights its emotional, historical, and structural complexity.

I shaped the interview approach to emphasize personal narrative, historical context, and emotional nuance, and I organized the final piece to guide viewers through the story in a clear and respectful way. This creative process helped me think carefully about how digital storytelling can humanize experiences that are often reduced to stereotypes or oversimplified public debate.

This project reflects my interest in using digital media and storytelling to humanize migration narratives and challenge stigmatized public discourse around citizenship.
